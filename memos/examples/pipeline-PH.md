## Thesis
Parker-Hannifin's FY2025 and Q3 FY2026 filings show gross-margin expansion (36.9% in FY2025 from 35.8%; 37.2% for nine-months FY2026 from 36.7%) despite roughly flat consolidated net sales, driven by price increases, favorable mix, and Win Strategy cost-containment initiatives. Aerospace Systems is a bright spot, with higher volumes and aftermarket profitability lifting segment gross margin even as Diversified Industrial faces mix and realignment headwinds. Net income attributable to shareholders rose to $3,531M in FY2025 from $2,844M, and Q3 FY2026 net sales grew year-over-year aided by both segments plus the Curtis acquisition, evidencing operating leverage and structural efficiency gains rather than purely cyclical tailwinds.

## Unresolved disagreements

- FY2025 net income growth to $3,531M from $2,844M may be materially inflated by a non-recurring gain on disposition of businesses rather than reflecting improved recurring operating earnings.
- Flat-to-declining FY2025 net sales ($19,850M vs $19,930M) mean the margin expansion was driven entirely by price and cost actions rather than volume growth, raising doubt about how durable the improvement is.
- Aerospace Systems is argued to be the sole driver of margin improvement while Diversified Industrial, the larger segment, is seeing margin decline from unfavorable mix and realignment charges, creating concentration risk if aerospace aftermarket demand normalizes.
- The nine-month FY2026 gross margin gain of 50bps is a deceleration versus FY2025's 110bps gain and is partly attributed to a lapping prior-year realignment benefit rather than purely organic improvement.
- The characterization of margin gains as 'structural' is disputed as resting on boilerplate strategy language rather than evidence, given that the cited drivers (price increases, cost containment, lapping realignment benefits) are viewed by the critique as cyclical or one-off.
- The FY2025 SG&A improvement is attributed partly to non-repeatable prior-year restructuring/integration comparisons and to lower R&D spending, which the critique flags as a potential earnings-quality concern rather than durable operating leverage.
