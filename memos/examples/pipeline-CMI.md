## Thesis
Cummins enters 2026 with resilient, diversified revenue (~$33.7B FY2025, roughly flat despite industrial headwinds), operating income of $4,025M implying an ~11.95% operating margin, and management actively cutting drag from underperforming Accelera electrolyzer operations. Tariff exposure has been immaterial to profitability, the Atmus split-off generated a ~$1.3B gain strengthening the balance sheet, and a ~640-location global distribution network with 13,000+ dealers across 190 countries provides earnings stability across cycles. Portfolio discipline (segment streamlining, divestitures) supports the operating-leverage/margin-expansion narrative even amid a slight top-line headwind.

## Bear case

1. **[material]** (re: claim 1) The claim frames flat net sales as evidence of resilience while ignoring that the same table shows net income attributable to Cummins collapsed from $3,946M to $2,843M (-28%) and diluted EPS from $28.37 to $20.50. Revenue stability is the least informative line here: earnings, not sales, deteriorated sharply. Moreover, the 2024 comparison base is itself inflated by the ~$1.3B Atmus split-off gain, so the true underlying earnings trajectory is obscured rather than 'stable.' Quarterly XBRL also shows deceleration within 2025 (Q2 $8,643M → Q3 $8,317M → implied Q4 ~$8,536M), and Q3 operating income fell to $852M from $1,226M in Q2 — a mid-year margin break, not a stable year.
   - > Net sales $ 33,670 $ 34,102 $ 34,065 Net income attributable to Cummins Inc. 2,843 3,946 735  
     `0000026172-26-000009#7¶8`
2. **[material]** (re: claim 2) The ~11.95% operating margin is not a clean read on operating quality: FY2025 operating income of $4,025M is struck after $458M of Accelera charges, and management explicitly footnotes them as included in results. Conversely, that margin cannot be compared to 2024 without adjusting for the $1.3B Atmus gain and 2024's own $312M of Accelera charges. Presenting 11.95% as 'consistent with the screener's reported margin level' conflates a charge-laden GAAP figure with normalized profitability, and the claim's own cited passage (¶8) does not contain any operating income or margin figure — the citation does not support the arithmetic asserted.
   - > Net income and earnings per common share included $458 million of charges for Accelera actions for the year ended December 31, 2025.  
     `0000026172-26-000009#7¶8`
3. **[material]** (re: claim 3) The tariff claim quotes only the reassuring half of management's sentence and omits the forward-looking warning that immediately follows it. The same paragraph states that continued tariff costs 'could materially and adversely affect' results, and management defers 2026 tariff impacts to the Outlook section — i.e., the 2025 immateriality is a backward-looking statement, not evidence that mitigation durably protects margins. The interpretive leap from 'immaterial in 2025' to 'effectively protecting margins from trade-policy volatility' is unsupported.

4. **[material]** (re: claim 4) Framing the electrolyzer exit as proactive 'portfolio discipline' inverts the filing's causation: management acted because of 'continued rapid deterioration' in electrolyzer and hydrogen markets and reductions in government incentives — a forced retreat, not a chosen optimization. This is also the second consecutive year of Accelera write-downs ($312M in Q4 2024, $458M in 2025, $770M combined), which undercuts the premise that the 2025 charges are a clean one-time reset. The exit is partial — 'We will continue to fulfill existing customer commitments' — so residual cost drag persists, and the filing quantifies no expected margin benefit.

## Unresolved disagreements

- The thesis reads flat FY2025 revenue as stability, but the critique notes net income fell 28% (to $2,843M from $3,946M) and diluted EPS dropped from $28.37 to $20.50 over the same period, with quarterly data showing a mid-year margin break rather than a stable year.
- The thesis treats the ~11.95% operating margin as a clean, comparable profitability metric, but the critique notes it is struck after $458M of Accelera charges and cannot be validly compared to a 2024 base that includes both a $1.3B Atmus gain and $312M of its own Accelera charges.
- The thesis frames 2025 tariff immateriality as evidence that mitigation is durably protecting margins, but the critique notes management's own filing warns that continued tariffs 'could materially and adversely affect' future results and defers 2026 tariff impact to the Outlook section.
- The thesis frames the electrolyzer exit as proactive portfolio discipline that will lift margins, but the critique notes the filing attributes the move to forced market deterioration and reduced government incentives, that it is only a partial exit with residual commitments, and that it is the second consecutive year of Accelera write-downs with no quantified margin benefit disclosed.
- The thesis cites the Atmus split-off gain as evidence of balance-sheet/capital strength, but the critique notes the gain was a non-cash, tax-free share exchange that raised no cash, and that the same period saw $1.9 billion in actual cash outflows under EPA/CARB settlement payments, with no net-debt disclosure in the cited passages.
