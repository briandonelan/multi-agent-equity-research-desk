## Thesis
Dover is a diversified industrial manufacturer (Engineered Products, Clean Energy & Fueling, Imaging & Identification, Pumps & Process Solutions, Climate & Sustainability Technologies) generating $8.1B trailing revenue at a ~17% operating margin and ~1.5x net debt/EBITDA. FY2025 showed accelerating revenue (+4.5%), gross margin expansion (+160bps to 39.8%) and operating-earnings growth outpacing sales (+13.8%), with bookings up 6.0% and Q1 2026 momentum building (revenue +10.1%, bookings +23.8%). However, the most recent quarter shows gross margin contracting 110bps YoY on portfolio mix, net earnings fell sharply on a one-off 2024 disposition gain comp, organic growth is materially softer than headline growth, and new euro-denominated debt is pushing interest expense higher — all of which complicate the 'clean improving-margin' read from the screen alone. Note: no true 2-10B mid-cap industrial matched the profitable/improving-margin screen; DOV ($28.5B market cap) was the smallest-cap, best-fitting name in the available universe.

## Unresolved disagreements

- Whether the FY2025 160bps gross-margin expansion is a durable trend or already reversing — Q1 2026 shows a 110bps YoY contraction attributed to portfolio mix, and the filings do not reconcile the two directions.
- Whether revenue/bookings acceleration reflects genuine organic demand strength or is substantially inflated by FX and M&A — FY2025 organic growth was 1.6% versus 4.5% headline, and Q1 2026 FX alone contributed 2.9 of the 10.1 points of growth.
- Whether the sharp decline in net earnings and earnings from continuing operations is purely a comp artifact from lapping the 2024 disposition gain, or also reflects genuine cost/margin pressure below the operating line — MD&A attributes it to the comp but does not fully explain the decline against 13.8% operating-earnings growth.
